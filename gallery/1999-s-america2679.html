1999 S America

011 - Favela childeren.

Image: 

"Listen to my radio." These 'barons' provided schools and entertainment for the people living in the favela, including the Samba schools preparing their dances and costumes for The Carnival.

013 - The Maracana Stadium.

Image: 

Botafogo v. Flamengo in the Maracana Stadium. In the evening we went to a local Torneio Rio-Sao Paulo football match. What an atmosphere. We supported Flamengo, where our hotel was, and also playing in red and black stripes. Romario was their striker. Every kick is cheered (or jeered), and they have all out parties when a goal is scored. We stood in the torrential rain all match, not minding at all as it was still 29C even at 10.30pm.

014 - Shoeshine boys.

Image: 

Shoeshine boys outside "Our Bar". We have become quite accepted down at our little bar, and we are well looked after by the owner and barman. We don't have to stray from our seats to get a beer or food, and they even vet who is allowed to come and speak to us, disposing of even the local prostitutes at the nod of a head from Bob or me.

015 - Rio de Janeiro at night from Sugar Loaf.

Image: 

Looking to Botafogo and Flamengo (Ash). We set off to Copocabana Beach then off to Botofogo to find an Indian restaurant. When we got there the Rajmahal had been sold by its original English owner, but the food was excellent, if a little pricey! We had decided to go up Sugar Loaf Mountain at night, so as to get spectacular views of Rio in the dark. It was amazing, and noone else was up there. It was a very hairy ride up 1,300 feet in an old Italian cable car. The mountain was first climbed by an english woman, a nanny, in 1817!

016 - Rio street carnival 1.

Image: 

Rio Carnival started today, Bob was feeling a little worse for wear, so I went off exploring on my own. Everywhere was closed, museums, galleries, shops, everything except the bars. Even the banks and post offices, even the British Consulate.

017 - Rio street carnival 2.

Image: 

Dancers in the Rio Branco. Not much of the day existed, we had got in at 6am and so didn't wake until mid-afternoon. We headed off to the Rio Branco to watch, and photograph the street carnival. All the costumes are made by the participants of the carnival. They can cost up to a months wages to produce, and are destroyed the day after.

018 - Rio Carnival 1.

Image: 

Carnival Floats in the Sambadromo. After watching the street parade we went into the Sambadromo for the worlds largest carnival. The Sambadromo was purpose built purely for this yearly event.

019 - Rio Carnival 2.

Image: 

Procession. The winning 'schools' from the previous years street procession are invited to take part in this years Sambadromo. Each dance school must produce its own floats, costumes, singers and song for the procession. They must process for ninety minutes playing the same song and are penalised for anything more or less than this. Seven 'schools' get this chance each year. It is a bizarre event, colour, feathers, glitter, music, special effects and thousands of people. It runs from 6pm to 6am and this years winners were 'The Salgueiro School'.

020 - Bob picks a chilli at a street stall.

Image: 

Street market stalls in Rio sell their wares & Bob chooses Chillies. We have been put into cook teams on the truck and before we leave Rio. Bob and I have to buy food for the next few days. We wandered the street markets where anything can be bought from books and clothes to spices and cacti.
