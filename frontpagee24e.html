112 - Sunset over Rio Chico Beach

Image: 

A long day's drive, not stopping in Caracas which is noted for it's violence. We camped on the Carribean coast itself, in hammocks slung between coconut palms on a white beach, a fire between us and the waves crashing on the beach all night. All we had for company were the pelicans, which flew over camp like we see geese in the UK.

111 - Cuidad Bolivar - Angostura Bridge

Image: 

Angostura (literally means narrows) was the old name for Cuidad Bolivar. This was the town on the banks of the Orinoco where Simon Bolivar based his liberation campaign against the Spanish. It was also the birth place of Angostura Bitters and is the only bridge across the whole length of the River Orinoco (opened in 1967). A very poor region of Venezuala it now mines gold, diamonds, iron, bauxite and oil.

110 - Angel Falls - Kerepacupai-meru

Image: 

Angel Falls itself was so 'grande'. A towering cliff face looming over you. Even today with so little water in, it is spectacular. The water dissipated into mist as it fell, only to reform and rain again into the plunge pool at the bottom. The pool is said to bestow 100years of life to anyone who bathes in it.
The tallest falls - 'falls from the deepest place' - in the world, at 979m, with a single drop of 807m, they are fed by the 700sqkm top of Auyan Tepui - 'the mountain of hell'.

109 - Tepui view

Image: 

Discovered by a venezualan naval officer in 1910, they are named after an american pilot, Jimmy Angel, who crashed onto the tepui in 1933, and survived.

108 - Jungle river view

Image: 

This must be one of the few unexplored places left on the earth.
Herons, egrets, pygmy kingfishers, cormorants and an otter sitting on a rock in the middle of the river, eating a fish.

107 - Canoe going up the rapids on the Churu river

Image: 

We set off north towards Angel Falls, dragging the boat 22 times over the rapids. It was tremendously hard work, but worth every second. "Hombre agua" and we would all have to jump out of the boat into the river, and pull. I broke my sandals, but the vertical sided mountains and the jungle and the river made it all worthwhile.
